CONTEXT & EVALUATION PHILOSOPHY:
Exercise affects glucose in complex ways — during aerobic exercise glucose typically drops, during high-intensity/anaerobic exercise it can spike due to cortisol/adrenaline. The post-workout window (especially 30–90 min after) carries delayed hypoglycaemia risk. We want to capture this pattern and surface it meaningfully.

--- WHAT TO STORE ON AN EXERCISE LOG ---
Ensure these fields exist on exercise log entries:
- exerciseType (string: e.g. "cardio", "strength", "HIIT", "yoga", "cycling", "run", etc.)
- durationMinutes (number)
- intensityLevel (low / moderate / high) — if not already captured, add this optional field to the log form
- startTime / endTime (or startTime + duration)
- glucoseBefore (fetched at log submit)
- glucoseAtEnd (fetched at workout end time)
- glucoseAfter1h (fetched +60min after end)

--- COLLAPSED VIEW for Exercise rows in Entry Log ---
Column order:
WHEN · TYPE (green dot + "EXERCISE") · DURATION (e.g. "45 min") · TYPE (e.g. "Cardio / Strength / HIIT") · outcome badge

Outcome badge for exercise should be one of:
- STABLE (green) — glucose within safe range before, during, and after
- DROPPED (amber) — glucose fell significantly post-workout
- SPIKED (red/orange) — glucose rose significantly (typical of anaerobic/HIIT)
- HYPO RISK (red) — post-workout glucose below 70 mg/dL
- PENDING (grey) — post-workout data not yet fetched

--- EXPANDED VIEW for Exercise logs ---

1. SESSION DETAILS
   - Exercise type, duration, intensity (if logged), start time, end time

2. GLUCOSE TRACKING
   - BG Before workout (fetched at log submit)
   - BG At End (fetched at workout end time) — "Pending [expected time]" if not yet
   - BG +1h post-end (fetched +60min after end) — same pending logic
   - Delta Before → At End and Before → +1h, colour coded
